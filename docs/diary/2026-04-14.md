## Quest: linkedin-block-art_2026-04-13__2232 (Abandoned)

**Outcome:** Abandoned after 12 iterations
**Branch:** linkedin (PR #76 closed)

### Context
Kjell wanted block art banners to render correctly when pasted from doc2md's LinkedIn view into LinkedIn posts. The in-app preview worked perfectly (monospace), but LinkedIn posts use a proportional font.

### What We Tried
1. Figure space (U+2007) replacement: spaces too narrow (8.67px vs 14px blocks)
2. Canvas-based per-column compensation: close but measurement didn't match LinkedIn exactly
3. Micro-correction after every character: over-padded
4. Segment-based alignment: broke the display
5. Em space (U+2003) replacement: best result, 0.15px/space drift
6. All-character normalization to █: pixel-perfect but all-black
7. Selective normalization (═ only, then += corners): good detail, small drift
8. Ideographic space (U+3000): collapsed by LinkedIn
9. Auto-selection between em and ideographic: function reference bug

### The Hard Ceiling
- █ = 14.00px (the only 14px block character)
- All detail characters (║╗╚╝═) = 13.75px
- Em space = 13.85px (best available gap)
- 0.25px/character drift is baked into the font
- Pixel-perfect + visual detail = impossible without monospace

### Human Inputs to Remember
- Kjell's standard: "not usable until pixel perfect"
- Kjell willing to spend significant time iterating toward quality
- Kjell provided LinkedIn DOM screenshots and console measurements (invaluable)
- Kjell suggested "smaller blocks but contiguous" as a creative angle

### Decisions
- Abandoned rather than shipping a "good enough" feature
- Knowledge banked for future: exact character width map for LinkedIn's font

### Open Questions
- Would LinkedIn articles (which support code blocks) work better?
- Could a custom block art font using only █ and spaces be aesthetically acceptable?
- Does LinkedIn's mobile app use different font metrics?

### Next Step
- None. Waiting for LinkedIn to support monospace in posts, or a creative breakthrough on proportional font alignment.
